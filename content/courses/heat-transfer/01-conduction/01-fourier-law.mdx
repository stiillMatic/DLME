# Fourier's Law of Heat Conduction

Heat flows from hot to cold. The rate of heat flow by conduction through a material is:

$$\dot{Q} = -kA\frac{dT}{dx}$$

where:
- $\dot{Q}$ = heat transfer rate (W)
- $k$ = thermal conductivity (W/m·K)
- $A$ = cross-sectional area (m²)
- $dT/dx$ = temperature gradient (K/m)

The minus sign ensures heat flows in the direction of decreasing temperature.

## Thermal Conductivities

| Material | $k$ (W/m·K) |
|---|---|
| Diamond | 2000 |
| Copper | 400 |
| Aluminum | 237 |
| Steel (carbon) | 50 |
| Glass | 1.0 |
| Wood | 0.15 |
| Air | 0.025 |
| Aerogel | 0.015 |

## 1D Steady-State Conduction: Flat Wall

For a wall of thickness $L$, thermal conductivity $k$, area $A$, with temperatures $T_1$ and $T_2$:

$$\dot{Q} = \frac{kA}{L}(T_1 - T_2) = \frac{T_1 - T_2}{R_{\text{cond}}}$$

**Thermal resistance:**

$$R_{\text{cond}} = \frac{L}{kA} \quad \left(\text{K/W}\right)$$

## Thermal Resistance Analogy

Heat flow $\leftrightarrow$ Electric current; Temperature difference $\leftrightarrow$ Voltage; Thermal resistance $\leftrightarrow$ Electrical resistance.

**Series resistances** (composite wall): $R_{\text{total}} = R_1 + R_2 + R_3$

**Parallel resistances**: $\frac{1}{R_{\text{total}}} = \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2}$

## Composite Wall Example

A wall consists of: 10 cm brick ($k = 0.7$ W/m·K) + 5 cm insulation ($k = 0.04$ W/m·K) + 1 cm drywall ($k = 0.17$ W/m·K). For $A = 1$ m²:

$$R_{\text{total}} = \frac{0.1}{0.7} + \frac{0.05}{0.04} + \frac{0.01}{0.17} = 0.143 + 1.25 + 0.059 = 1.45\ \text{K/W}$$

## Practice Problems

<ExQ>
A steel plate ($k = 50$ W/m·K) is 20 mm thick and has area 0.5 m². One face is at 200°C, the other at 50°C. Find the heat transfer rate.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$$\dot{Q} = \frac{kA(T_1 - T_2)}{L} = \frac{50 \times 0.5 \times (200 - 50)}{0.02} = \frac{50 \times 0.5 \times 150}{0.02} = \boxed{187{,}500\ \text{W} = 187.5\ \text{kW}}$$
</ExA>

<ExQ>
A composite wall (1 m² area) has two layers in series: Layer 1 is 5 cm aluminum ($k = 237$ W/m·K), Layer 2 is 3 cm glass ($k = 1.0$ W/m·K). The outer temperatures are 100°C and 20°C. Find the total heat flux and the interface temperature.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Thermal resistances:
$$R_1 = \frac{0.05}{237 \times 1} = 2.11 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{K/W}, \quad R_2 = \frac{0.03}{1.0 \times 1} = 0.03\ \text{K/W}$$

$$R_{\text{total}} \approx 0.0302\ \text{K/W}$$

$$\dot{Q} = \frac{100 - 20}{0.0302} = \boxed{2649\ \text{W}}$$

Interface temperature (after layer 1):
$$T_{\text{int}} = 100 - \dot{Q} \times R_1 = 100 - 2649 \times 2.11\times10^{-4} \approx \boxed{99.4°\text{C}}$$

(Almost all resistance is in the glass layer.)
</ExA>
