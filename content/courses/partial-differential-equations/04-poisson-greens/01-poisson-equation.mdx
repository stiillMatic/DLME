# Poisson's Equation and Laplace's Equation

**Poisson's equation:**

$$\nabla^2 u = f(\mathbf{x})$$

**Laplace's equation** (when $f = 0$):

$$\nabla^2 u = 0$$

In 2D Cartesian: $\nabla^2 u = \dfrac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} + \dfrac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2}$

In cylindrical: $\nabla^2 u = \dfrac{1}{r}\dfrac{\partial}{\partial r}\!\left(r\dfrac{\partial u}{\partial r}\right) + \dfrac{1}{r^2}\dfrac{\partial^2 u}{\partial\theta^2}$

## Physical Problems Governed by These Equations

| Problem | $u$ | $f$ |
|---|---|---|
| Steady heat conduction | Temperature $T$ | $-Q/k$ (heat source) |
| Electrostatics | Electric potential $\phi$ | $-\rho/\varepsilon_0$ (charge density) |
| Groundwater flow | Hydraulic head $h$ | Source/sink rate |
| Membrane deflection | Deflection $w$ | $-p/T$ (pressure / tension) |
| 2D potential flow | Velocity potential $\phi$ | 0 (Laplace) |

## Boundary Conditions

Three types:
- **Dirichlet:** $u = g$ on boundary (prescribed value — e.g., fixed temperature)
- **Neumann:** $\partial u/\partial n = h$ on boundary (prescribed flux — e.g., insulated wall: $\partial T/\partial n = 0$)
- **Robin:** $\partial u/\partial n + \alpha u = h$ (convection BC in heat transfer)

## Properties of Harmonic Functions

Solutions to $\nabla^2 u = 0$ are **harmonic** and satisfy:
- **Mean value property:** $u$ at any point equals the average over any sphere centered there
- **Maximum principle:** $u$ attains its max and min on the boundary (never in the interior)
- **Uniqueness:** Dirichlet and Neumann problems have unique solutions (up to a constant for pure Neumann)

## Steady-State Heat Equation Example

A 2D plate with $\nabla^2 T = 0$, $T = 0$ on three sides, $T = T_0\sin(\pi x/L)$ on the top edge $y=H$:

$$T(x,y) = T_0\sin\!\left(\frac{\pi x}{L}\right)\frac{\sinh(\pi y/L)}{\sinh(\pi H/L)}$$

## Practice Problems

<ExQ>
Verify that $u(x,y) = e^x\cos y$ is harmonic, and find a physical context for this solution.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$$\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} = e^x\cos y, \quad \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2} = -e^x\cos y$$

$$\nabla^2 u = e^x\cos y - e^x\cos y = 0 \checkmark$$

**Physical context:** In 2D potential flow, $\phi = e^x\cos y$ is a velocity potential. The complex potential is $w(z) = e^z = e^x(\cos y + i\sin y)$, representing an exponential flow. In heat transfer, $u = e^x\cos y$ could be the temperature in a plate with specific boundary conditions.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
A long square rod (cross-section $[0,1]\times[0,1]$) has $T=0$ on three sides and $T=100$ on the top. Write the formal series solution.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Separate variables: $T = X(x)Y(y)$, with $X(0)=X(1)=0$ and $Y(0)=0$.

$$X_n = \sin(n\pi x), \quad Y_n = \sinh(n\pi y)$$

General solution: $T = \sum_{n=1}^\infty A_n\sin(n\pi x)\sinh(n\pi y)$

Top BC $T(x,1)=100$: $\sum A_n\sin(n\pi x)\sinh(n\pi) = 100$

Fourier sine coefficients:
$$A_n = \frac{2}{\sinh(n\pi)}\int_0^1 100\sin(n\pi x)\,dx = \frac{200(1-\cos n\pi)}{n\pi\sinh(n\pi)}$$

Non-zero for odd $n$ only: $A_n = \dfrac{400}{n\pi\sinh(n\pi)}$ for $n = 1, 3, 5, \ldots$
</ExA>
