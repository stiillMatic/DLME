# Chapter 2 Practice — Spontaneity and Free Energy

<ExQ>
**1.** $\Delta H = +50$ kJ, $\Delta S = +200$ J/K. Spontaneous at 200 K? 300 K?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$T = 200$: $\Delta G = 50 - 40 = +10$ kJ. Non-spontaneous.

$T = 300$: $\Delta G = 50 - 60 = -10$ kJ. Spontaneous.

Crossover: $T = 50/0.2 = 250$ K. Heating drives this endothermic reaction.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**2.** $K = 100$ for a reaction. $\Delta G^\circ$ at 25°C?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\Delta G^\circ = -RT\ln K = -8.314\cdot 298\cdot\ln 100 \approx -11.4$ kJ/mol.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**3.** Acetone $\Delta H_{\text{vap}} = 31.3$ kJ/mol, normal boiling 56°C. Boiling at 0.3 atm?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\ln(0.3) = -3768\cdot(1/T_2 - 1/329)$.

$-1.204/-3768 = 3.2\times 10^{-4}$.

$1/T_2 = 1/329 + 3.2\times 10^{-4} = 3.36\times 10^{-3}$.

$T_2 \approx 298$ K ≈ 25°C.

At reduced pressure, acetone boils at room temperature — basis of vacuum distillation in pharma.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**4. Engineering: Lead-acid battery.** PbO₂ + Pb + 2H₂SO₄ → 2PbSO₄ + 2H₂O. If $\Delta G^\circ = -397$ kJ/mol (per 2 electrons), cell voltage?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$E^\circ = -\Delta G^\circ/(nF) = 397000/(2\cdot 96485) \approx 2.06$ V per cell.

A 12 V car battery: 6 cells in series.

Real voltage drops with discharge as reactants deplete. Sulfate buildup eventually limits usability.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**5. Conceptual.** Why is $\Delta G$ so useful at constant T, P, when $\Delta U$ is more fundamental?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\Delta G$ packages everything you need for spontaneity at lab conditions (constant T, P) into one number — and equals maximum useful work.

$\Delta U$ requires you to also consider heat and PV work separately, plus surroundings' entropy.

Practical chemistry: use $\Delta G$. Theoretical statistical mechanics: $\Delta U$ + microstate counts.

This is a good example of choosing the right state function for the conditions. Helmholtz $A = U - TS$ is preferred at constant T,V; Gibbs at constant T,P; etc.
</ExA>
