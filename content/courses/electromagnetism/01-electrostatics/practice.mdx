# Chapter 1 Practice — Electrostatics

<ExQ>
**1.** Field 1 cm from a 1 nC charge?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$E = (10^{-9})/(4\pi\cdot 8.85\times 10^{-12}\cdot 10^{-4}) = 9000$ V/m.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**2.** Force between an electron and proton in hydrogen atom (separation 0.05 nm).
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$F = (1.6\times 10^{-19})^2/(4\pi\cdot 8.85\times 10^{-12}\cdot (5\times 10^{-11})^2) = 9.2\times 10^{-8}$ N.

(Tiny in absolute terms but huge per atom.)
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**3. Gauss's law.** Spherical shell of charge $Q$, radius $R$. Field inside? Outside?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
**Inside (r < R):** by Gauss, no enclosed charge → $E = 0$.

**Outside (r > R):** $E = Q/(4\pi\varepsilon_0 r^2)$ — same as point charge at center.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**4. Conceptual.** Why is the electric field inside a conductor in electrostatic equilibrium zero?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
If $E \neq 0$ inside, free electrons would accelerate, redistributing charge until they don't. Equilibrium ⟹ $E = 0$ inside.

Excess charges sit on the surface — basis of Faraday cages and shielding.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**5. Engineering.** Air breaks down at $E = 3$ MV/m. Find max voltage on a parallel-plate capacitor with 0.5 mm gap.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$V_{\max} = E_{\max}\cdot d = 3\times 10^6\cdot 5\times 10^{-4} = 1500$ V.

This sets the operating limit for high-voltage components in air.
</ExA>
