# Chapter 2 Practice — Magnetism

<ExQ>
**1.** Charge 2 μC at $10^4$ m/s perpendicular to $B = 0.1$ T. Force?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$F = qvB = 2\times 10^{-6}\cdot 10^4\cdot 0.1 = 2\times 10^{-3}$ N.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**2.** Cyclotron with $B = 1$ T, proton accelerated. After many revolutions, $r = 0.5$ m. Speed?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$v = qBr/m = 1.6\times 10^{-19}\cdot 1\cdot 0.5/1.67\times 10^{-27} = 4.79\times 10^7$ m/s.

About 16% of c. Energy ≈ ½mv² ≈ 12 MeV. Standard for medical isotope production.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**3.** Coil 100 turns, area 0.05 m², in $B$ that decreases at 0.2 T/s. EMF?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$|\varepsilon| = N\cdot A\cdot dB/dt = 100\cdot 0.05\cdot 0.2 = 1$ V.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**4. Engineering: Transformer for wall adapter.** 120 V AC primary needs to produce 12 V DC. Ratio of turns?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Transformer step-down: $N_1/N_2 = V_1/V_2 = 120/12 = 10$. Primary has 10× more turns.

(DC requires rectification after transformer — diodes convert AC to DC.)

This is the principle of every wall-wart power adapter.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**5. Conceptual.** Why does an aluminum disk dropped through a magnet's field slow down (eddy current braking)?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
As disk enters field, flux through it changes → induced EMF → eddy currents in disk.

By Lenz's law: induced currents create magnetic field opposing the change → disk experiences upward force opposing motion.

The kinetic energy converts to heat (resistive dissipation in disk).

Used in: roller coaster braking, train braking, induction heating, metal sorting (separating aluminum cans by eddy current).

Pure mechanical advantage: no contact, no wear, instant response.
</ExA>
