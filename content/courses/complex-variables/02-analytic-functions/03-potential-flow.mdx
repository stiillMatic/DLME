# Application: Potential Flow in Fluid Mechanics

For 2D, incompressible, irrotational flow, the velocity field $\mathbf{V} = (u, v)$ satisfies:

$$\nabla^2\phi = 0 \quad \text{(velocity potential)}$$

where $u = \partial\phi/\partial x$ and $v = \partial\phi/\partial y$.

## The Complex Potential

Define the **complex potential** $w(z) = \phi(x,y) + i\psi(x,y)$, where:
- $\phi$ = velocity potential
- $\psi$ = streamfunction (streamlines are curves $\psi = \text{const}$)

Since $w$ is analytic, the C–R equations give exactly:

$$\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial\psi}{\partial y} = u, \qquad \frac{\partial\phi}{\partial y} = -\frac{\partial\psi}{\partial x} = v$$

The **complex velocity** is:

$$\frac{dw}{dz} = u - iv$$

## Standard Flow Solutions

| Flow | Complex potential | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Uniform flow (speed $U$) | $w = Uz$ | Parallel streamlines |
| Source/sink (strength $m$) | $w = \frac{m}{2\pi}\ln z$ | Radial outflow from origin |
| Vortex (circulation $\Gamma$) | $w = -\frac{i\Gamma}{2\pi}\ln z$ | Circular streamlines |
| Doublet | $w = \frac{\mu}{2\pi z}$ | Pair of source+sink |
| Flow past a cylinder | $w = U\!\left(z + \frac{a^2}{z}\right)$ | Superposition: uniform + doublet |

## Flow Past a Circular Cylinder

The complex potential $w = U(z + a^2/z)$ gives irrotational flow around a cylinder of radius $a$. On the cylinder surface $|z| = a$:

$$\psi = 0 \quad \text{(cylinder is a streamline)}$$

The velocity on the surface: $|dw/dz| = 2U|\sin\theta|$. Stagnation points at $\theta = 0, \pi$.

By Bernoulli, pressure varies around the cylinder — the starting point for understanding aerodynamic drag and lift.

## Conformal Mapping

Analytic functions map curves to curves, **preserving angles**. The Joukowski transform $z \mapsto z + c^2/z$ maps a circle to an airfoil profile — a powerful technique for solving flow over complex geometries by mapping them to simpler ones.

## Practice Problems

<ExQ>
For uniform flow with complex potential $w = Uz$ ($U > 0$ real), find the velocity components $u$ and $v$, and sketch the streamlines.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$$\frac{dw}{dz} = U = u - iv \implies u = U,\quad v = 0$$

The flow is purely horizontal at speed $U$. Streamlines are horizontal lines $y = \text{const}$ (since $\psi = Uy$).
</ExA>

<ExQ>
A source of strength $m = 2\pi$ is placed at the origin. Find the radial velocity at distance $r = 2$ from the origin.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$w = \frac{m}{2\pi}\ln z = \ln z$

$$\frac{dw}{dz} = \frac{1}{z} = \frac{1}{re^{i\theta}} = \frac{e^{-i\theta}}{r}$$

At $r=2$: $|dw/dz| = 1/2$. The velocity magnitude is $1/2$, directed radially outward.

Radial velocity: $v_r = m/(2\pi r) = 1/2$.
</ExA>
