# Residues and the Residue Theorem

When $f(z)$ has an isolated singularity at $z_0$, its Laurent series has the form:

$$f(z) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n (z-z_0)^n$$

The coefficient $a_{-1}$ is the **residue** of $f$ at $z_0$:

$$\text{Res}(f, z_0) = a_{-1}$$

## Types of Singularities

| Type | Laurent series | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Removable | No negative powers | $\sin z / z$ at $z=0$ |
| Simple pole (order 1) | $a_{-1}/(z-z_0) + \ldots$ | $1/(z-1)$ at $z=1$ |
| Pole of order $m$ | $a_{-m}/(z-z_0)^m + \ldots$ | $1/(z-1)^3$ at $z=1$ |
| Essential | Infinitely many negative powers | $e^{1/z}$ at $z=0$ |

## Computing Residues

**Simple pole at $z_0$:**
$$\text{Res}(f, z_0) = \lim_{z\to z_0}(z-z_0)f(z)$$

**Pole of order $m$:**
$$\text{Res}(f, z_0) = \frac{1}{(m-1)!}\lim_{z\to z_0}\frac{d^{m-1}}{dz^{m-1}}\left[(z-z_0)^m f(z)\right]$$

**Ratio $p/q$ with simple zero of $q$ at $z_0$:**
$$\text{Res}\!\left(\frac{p}{q}, z_0\right) = \frac{p(z_0)}{q'(z_0)}$$

## Residue Theorem

If $f$ is analytic inside and on $C$ except at isolated singularities $z_1, \ldots, z_k$ inside $C$:

$$\oint_C f(z)\,dz = 2\pi i \sum_{j=1}^k \text{Res}(f, z_j)$$

This reduces any contour integral to computing a few residues â€” enormously powerful.

## Practice Problems

<ExQ>
Find the residue of $f(z) = \dfrac{e^z}{(z-1)^2}$ at $z = 1$.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Pole of order 2 at $z = 1$:

$$\text{Res}(f, 1) = \lim_{z\to 1}\frac{d}{dz}\left[(z-1)^2 \cdot \frac{e^z}{(z-1)^2}\right] = \lim_{z\to 1}\frac{d}{dz}e^z = e^1 = \boxed{e}$$
</ExA>

<ExQ>
Evaluate $\displaystyle\oint_C \frac{z^2}{(z-1)(z+2)}\,dz$ where $C$ is $|z| = 3$.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Both poles $z = 1$ and $z = -2$ are inside $|z| = 3$.

**Res at $z=1$:**
$$\lim_{z\to 1}(z-1)\cdot\frac{z^2}{(z-1)(z+2)} = \frac{1}{3}$$

**Res at $z=-2$:**
$$\lim_{z\to -2}(z+2)\cdot\frac{z^2}{(z-1)(z+2)} = \frac{4}{-3} = -\frac{4}{3}$$

$$\oint_C = 2\pi i\left(\frac{1}{3} - \frac{4}{3}\right) = 2\pi i(-1) = \boxed{-2\pi i}$$
</ExA>

<ExQ>
Find the residue of $\dfrac{\cos z}{z^2}$ at $z = 0$ using the Laurent series.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Laurent series of $\cos z / z^2$ around $z = 0$:

$$\frac{\cos z}{z^2} = \frac{1}{z^2}\left(1 - \frac{z^2}{2!} + \frac{z^4}{4!} - \cdots\right) = \frac{1}{z^2} - \frac{1}{2} + \frac{z^2}{24} - \cdots$$

The coefficient of $1/z$ is **0**. Therefore $\text{Res}(\cos z/z^2, 0) = \boxed{0}$.

(There is a pole of order 2, but the residue â€” the $a_{-1}$ coefficient â€” happens to be zero.)
</ExA>
