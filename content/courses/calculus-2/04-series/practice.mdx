# Chapter 4 Practice — Sequences and Series

<ExQ>
**1.** Does $\sum 1/(n\sqrt n)$ converge?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$p$-series with $p = 3/2 > 1$. Converges.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**2.** Does $\sum \cos(n\pi)/n$ converge?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\cos(n\pi) = (-1)^n$, so this is alternating harmonic. Converges (conditionally).
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**3.** Find $R$ for $\sum x^n/n^2$.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$|a_n/a_{n+1}| = (n+1)^2/n^2 \to 1$. $R = 1$.

Endpoints: $x = 1$: $\sum 1/n^2$ converges. $x = -1$: also converges (absolute).

Interval: $[-1, 1]$.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**4.** Compute first 4 terms of Maclaurin series for $\arctan x$.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\arctan' = 1/(1+x^2) = \sum(-1)^n x^{2n}$.

Integrate: $\arctan x = \sum (-1)^n x^{2n+1}/(2n+1) = x - x^3/3 + x^5/5 - x^7/7 + \cdots$.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**5. Engineering: Pi computation.** Set $x = 1$ in $\arctan$ series: $\pi/4 = 1 - 1/3 + 1/5 - \cdots$. How many terms for 4 decimals?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Alternating series error bound: $|R_N| \leq 1/(2N+3)$.

Need $1/(2N+3) < 5\times 10^{-5}$, i.e., $N > 10000$.

So **about 10,000 terms** for 4 decimal places. Slow! This is why Machin-like formulas are used for fast π computation.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**6. Conceptual.** Why does $\arctan x$ series only converge on $[-1, 1]$ even though $\arctan x$ is defined for all real $x$?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\arctan x = \int 1/(1+t^2)\,dt$. The integrand has singularities at $t = \pm i$ — distance 1 from 0 in complex plane.

Power series radius of convergence = distance to nearest singularity (in complex plane). Hence $R = 1$.

Outside $|x| = 1$, the *series* diverges, but $\arctan x$ itself is fine. Just need a different representation (e.g., series around a different center).
</ExA>
