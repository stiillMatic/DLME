# Chapter 2 Practice — Applications of Integration

<ExQ>
**1.** Volume from rotating $y = \sqrt x$ on $[0, 4]$ around $x$-axis.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$V = \pi\int_0^4 x\,dx = 8\pi$.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**2.** Arc length of $y = x^{3/2}$ from 0 to 1.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$y' = (3/2)\sqrt x$. $1 + (y')^2 = 1 + 9x/4$.

$L = \int_0^1\sqrt{1 + 9x/4}\,dx$. Let $u = 1 + 9x/4$.

$L = (4/9)(2/3)[u^{3/2}]_1^{13/4} = (8/27)((13/4)^{3/2} - 1) \approx 1.44$.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**3.** Surface area of paraboloid: rotate $y = x^2$ on $[0, 1]$ around $y$-axis.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$S = \int_0^1 2\pi x\sqrt{1+(2x)^2}\,dx$. Let $u = 1+4x^2, du = 8x\,dx$.

$S = (\pi/4)\int_1^5 \sqrt u\,du = (\pi/6)(5^{3/2} - 1) \approx 5.33$.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**4. Work.** Spring force $F = 50x$ N. Work to stretch from $0.1$ to $0.3$ m.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$W = 25(0.09 - 0.01) = 2$ J.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**5. Engineering: Pumping.** Conical tank (apex down, half-angle 45°), full to height 4 m. Work to pump out?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
At height $y$ from apex, radius $r = y$. Layer volume $= \pi y^2\,dy$, mass = $1000\pi y^2\,dy$, weight = $9810\pi y^2\,dy$ N.

Lifting distance to top = $4 - y$.

$W = \int_0^4 9810\pi y^2(4-y)\,dy = 9810\pi[4y^3/3 - y^4/4]_0^4 = 9810\pi(256/3 - 64) = 9810\pi\cdot 64/3$

$= 657{,}600$ J ≈ 658 kJ.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**6. Conceptual.** Why doesn't $V = \pi r^2 h$ give correct volume for cones?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
That formula is for cylinders. Cones taper — radius varies along axis. Need integration: $V = \int_0^h \pi r(z)^2\,dz$.

For a cone: $r(z) = (R/h)z$ (or $R - (R/h)z$ depending on orientation). Then $V = \pi R^2 h/3$ — one-third of the cylinder.

This $1/3$ factor is geometric: the cone fills 1/3 of the bounding cylinder.
</ExA>
