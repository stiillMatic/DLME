# Chapter 3 Practice — Bound States

<ExQ>
**1.** Electron in 2 nm well. $E_2 - E_1$?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$E_n = (\hbar^2\pi^2/2m_e L^2)n^2$. $E_2 - E_1 = 3E_1$.

$E_1 \approx 0.094$ eV (electron, $L = 2$ nm).

$\Delta E \approx 0.28$ eV.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**2.** HO with $E_0 = 0.5$ eV. Next level energy?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Spacing $\hbar\omega = 2E_0 = 1$ eV. $E_1 = 1.5$ eV.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**3. Engineering: Vibrational frequency of HCl.** Reduced mass $\mu = 1.63\times 10^{-27}$ kg, spring constant 481 N/m. Find $\omega$, $E_0$.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\omega = \sqrt{k/\mu} = \sqrt{481/1.63\times 10^{-27}} \approx 5.43\times 10^{14}$ rad/s.

$E_0 = \hbar\omega/2 \approx 2.87\times 10^{-20}$ J ≈ 0.179 eV.

IR absorption near $\omega/(2\pi) \approx 8.6\times 10^{13}$ Hz, $\lambda \approx 3.5$ μm. Matches HCl IR spectrum.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**4. Conceptual.** Why does HO have equally spaced levels but the infinite well doesn't?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
HO potential is $\propto x^2$: at high $n$, the classical period stays roughly constant — same time scale → same energy spacing.

Infinite well: as $n$ grows, particle is faster (higher kinetic energy at same well width) → period shorter → larger energy difference.

The functional form of $V(x)$ controls the spectrum's spacing pattern.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**5.** Probability of finding HO ground-state particle classically forbidden region $|x| > \sqrt{\hbar/m\omega}$?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
For ground state, classical turning points at $E_0 = \tfrac{1}{2}m\omega^2 x_{\text{cl}}^2 \Rightarrow x_{\text{cl}} = \sqrt{\hbar/m\omega}$.

Wavefunction: Gaussian $\phi_0\propto e^{-m\omega x^2/(2\hbar)}$.

Probability outside classical region: 2× P(x > $x_{\text{cl}}$) ≈ 15.7%.

Significant probability of being where classically forbidden — characteristic quantum feature, related to tunneling.
</ExA>
