# Chapter 3 Practice — Thermochemistry

<ExQ>
**1.** Enthalpy of formation: H₂O(l) = -286, CO₂ = -394, glucose = -1273 kJ/mol.

Find $\Delta H$ for $\text{C}_6\text{H}_{12}\text{O}_6 + 6\text{O}_2 \to 6\text{CO}_2 + 6\text{H}_2\text{O}$.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\Delta H = 6(-394) + 6(-286) - (-1273) - 0 = -4080 + 1273 = -2807$ kJ/mol.

(About 16 kJ/g — close to actual food-energy of carbs.)
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**2.** Spontaneous if $\Delta G < 0$. Reaction: $\Delta H = -50$ kJ, $\Delta S = -100$ J/K. Spontaneous at 300 K? At 600 K?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
At 300 K: $\Delta G = -50000 - 300\cdot(-100) = -50000 + 30000 = -20$ kJ. Spontaneous.

At 600 K: $\Delta G = -50000 + 60000 = +10$ kJ. **Not** spontaneous.

Crossover: $T = \Delta H/\Delta S = 500$ K. Above this, the reaction reverses.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**3.** Combustion of 1 g methane releases 55.7 MJ/kg = 55.7 kJ/g. How much steam (at 100°C → 100°C, $\Delta H_{\text{vap}} = 2260$ kJ/kg) can be vaporized?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$m_{\text{steam}} = 55.7/2260 = 0.0246$ kg = 24.6 g per gram of methane.

(Real boilers achieve ~80% efficiency: ~20 g steam per g methane.)
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**4. Conceptual.** Why does dissolution of NH₄NO₃ in water lower the temperature even though it's spontaneous?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\Delta H_{\text{soln}} > 0$ (endothermic — absorbs heat).

But $\Delta S_{\text{soln}} > 0$ (more disorder when ions disperse) is large enough that $\Delta G = \Delta H - T\Delta S < 0$.

Driven by entropy. Heat absorbed comes from the water — temperature drops. Used in cold packs.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**5. Multi-step.** $2\text{Al} + \tfrac{3}{2}\text{O}_2 \to \text{Al}_2\text{O}_3$, $\Delta H = -1676$ kJ/mol. Used in thermite reaction with Fe₂O₃: $2\text{Al} + \text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3 \to \text{Al}_2\text{O}_3 + 2\text{Fe}$. Given $\Delta H_f^\circ$(Fe₂O₃) = -824 kJ/mol, find thermite $\Delta H$.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\Delta H = (-1676) + 0 - 0 - (-824) = -852$ kJ/mol.

Massive heat release — thermite reaches over 2500°C, melts iron. Used to weld railroad tracks.
</ExA>
