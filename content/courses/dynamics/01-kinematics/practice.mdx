# Chapter 1 Practice — Kinematics

<ExQ>
**1.** Position $s = 3t^2 - 12t$ m. Find $v(2)$ and $a(2)$.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$v = 6t - 12$. $v(2) = 0$. $a = 6$ m/s² (constant).

(Particle is momentarily at rest at $t = 2$ but still accelerating.)
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**2.** Particle on circular path, radius 5 m, speed 10 m/s, decelerating at 2 m/s². Find total acceleration.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$a_t = -2$, $a_n = 100/5 = 20$.

$|a| = \sqrt{4 + 400} \approx 20.1$ m/s².
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**3. Engineering: Banking.** A road banked at 10° is to allow 30 m/s with no friction (radius $R$). Find $R$. ($\tan\theta = v^2/(gR)$.)
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$R = v^2/(g\tan\theta) = 900/(9.81\cdot 0.176) = 521$ m.

Highway curves designed so the banking matches the design speed.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**4. Conceptual.** Why does a satellite in circular orbit have $|\mathbf{a}| = g_{\text{eff}}$?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
The only force is gravity, providing centripetal acceleration. By Newton's second law: $a = F/m = g_{\text{eff}}$.

For circular orbit: $g = v^2/r \Rightarrow v = \sqrt{gr}$. This determines orbital speed entirely.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**5.** Polar coords: $r = 2t$, $\theta = t^2$. Find $v_r$ and $v_\theta$ at $t = 1$.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\dot r = 2$, $\dot\theta = 2t = 2$. $r(1) = 2$.

$v_r = \dot r = 2$ m/s.
$v_\theta = r\dot\theta = 4$ m/s.
$|\mathbf{v}| = \sqrt{4 + 16} = \sqrt{20} \approx 4.47$ m/s.
</ExA>
