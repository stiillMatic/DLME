# Normal and Shear Stress

**Stress** is force per unit area. It quantifies the internal intensity of force within a material.

## Normal Stress

Normal stress $\sigma$ acts perpendicular to the cross-sectional area:

$$\sigma = \frac{P}{A}$$

where $P$ is the axial force and $A$ is the cross-sectional area.

- **Tensile stress** ($P$ pulls): $\sigma > 0$
- **Compressive stress** ($P$ pushes): $\sigma < 0$

Units: Pa = N/m², MPa = N/mm²

## Shear Stress

Shear stress $\tau$ acts **parallel** to the cross-section:

$$\tau = \frac{V}{A}$$

where $V$ is the shear force. (This is average shear — real distributions are parabolic for beams.)

## Stress on Inclined Planes

For an axially loaded bar, on a plane inclined at angle $\theta$ from the cross-section:

$$\sigma_\theta = \sigma_0 \cos^2\theta, \qquad \tau_\theta = \frac{\sigma_0}{2}\sin 2\theta$$

**Maximum shear stress** occurs at $\theta = 45°$: $\tau_{\max} = \sigma_0/2$

This is why ductile materials in tension fail at 45° — it's the plane of maximum shear.

## Factor of Safety

$$n = \frac{\sigma_{\text{allowable}}}{\sigma_{\text{actual}}}$$

Typical design factors: $n = 1.5$–$4$ depending on application and consequence of failure.

## 2D Stress State (General)

At a point in a body, the full stress state requires:

$$\boldsymbol{\sigma} = \begin{pmatrix} \sigma_x & \tau_{xy} \\ \tau_{xy} & \sigma_y \end{pmatrix}$$

Eigenvalues of this matrix are the **principal stresses** $\sigma_1, \sigma_2$.

## Practice Problems

<ExQ>
A steel rod of diameter 20 mm carries a tensile load of 30 kN. Find the normal stress. Is it safe if the allowable stress is 150 MPa?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$$A = \frac{\pi d^2}{4} = \frac{\pi (0.02)^2}{4} = 3.14 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{m}^2$$

$$\sigma = \frac{P}{A} = \frac{30{,}000}{3.14 \times 10^{-4}} = 95.5\ \text{MPa}$$

Since $95.5 < 150$ MPa, the rod is **safe**. Factor of safety: $n = 150/95.5 = 1.57$.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
An axially loaded bar has normal stress $\sigma_0 = 80$ MPa. Find the shear stress on the plane inclined at $\theta = 30°$ from the cross-section.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$$\tau_\theta = \frac{\sigma_0}{2}\sin 2\theta = \frac{80}{2}\sin 60° = 40 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = \boxed{34.6\ \text{MPa}}$$
</ExA>

<ExQ>
A bolt of cross-sectional area 100 mm² is in double shear, carrying a total force of 20 kN. Find the average shear stress in the bolt.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
In double shear, the force is shared across **two** shear planes:

$$\tau = \frac{V}{2A} = \frac{20{,}000}{2 \times 100} = \boxed{100\ \text{MPa}}$$
</ExA>
