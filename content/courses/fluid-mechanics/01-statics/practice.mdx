# Chapter 1 Practice — Fluid Statics

<ExQ>
**1.** Pressure at 5 m depth in oil ($\rho = 850$ kg/m³)?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$p = 850\cdot 9.81\cdot 5 = 41.7$ kPa.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**2.** A 2 m × 3 m vertical wall is submerged with top edge at the water surface. Find total force.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Centroid at depth $1$ m. $p_c = 9810$ Pa.
$F = p_c A = 9810\cdot 6 = 58.86$ kN.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**3.** A 0.5 m³ block submerged in water. Find buoyant force.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$F_B = \rho_w g V = 1000\cdot 9.81\cdot 0.5 = 4.91$ kN.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**4. Conceptual.** Why doesn't pressure depend on lateral position in a static fluid?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
At a given depth, force balance horizontally requires equal pressure on all sides — otherwise the fluid would flow. So pressure is constant on horizontal surfaces.

It only varies vertically (with depth) due to gravity.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**5. Engineering.** A submarine of mass 1500 t and total displacement 1525 m³. Is it sinking or rising?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Buoyancy: $\rho_{\text{sea}}g V = 1025\cdot 9.81\cdot 1525 = 15.34$ MN.

Weight: $1.5\times 10^6\cdot 9.81 = 14.72$ MN.

Net upward = 0.62 MN → **rising** (positive buoyancy).

To stay at depth: needs to take on water (or use thrusters to hold position).
</ExA>
