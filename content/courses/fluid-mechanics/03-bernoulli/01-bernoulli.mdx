# Bernoulli's Equation

For steady, incompressible, inviscid flow along a streamline:

$$P + \frac{1}{2}\rho v^2 + \rho g z = \text{constant}$$

where $P$ = pressure, $\rho$ = density, $v$ = velocity, $z$ = elevation.

Equivalently, between two points 1 and 2 on the same streamline:

$$P_1 + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_1^2 + \rho g z_1 = P_2 + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_2^2 + \rho g z_2$$

## Physical Interpretation

- **Pressure term** $P$: flow work per unit volume
- **Kinetic term** $\frac{1}{2}\rho v^2$: kinetic energy per unit volume
- **Potential term** $\rho g z$: gravitational potential per unit volume

When velocity increases, pressure decreases — and vice versa. This is why airplane wings generate lift.

## Continuity Equation

For incompressible flow in a pipe:

$$A_1 v_1 = A_2 v_2 = \dot{V} = \text{const}$$

Smaller pipe area → higher velocity. Combined with Bernoulli → lower pressure.

## Venturi Meter

A Venturi meter measures flow rate using a constriction. At the throat (section 2):

$$\dot{V} = A_2 \sqrt{\frac{2(P_1 - P_2)/\rho}{1 - (A_2/A_1)^2}}$$

## Pitot Tube

Measures flow velocity by stagnating the flow ($v_2 = 0$, same elevation):

$$P_{\text{stag}} - P_{\text{static}} = \frac{1}{2}\rho v^2 \implies v = \sqrt{\frac{2\Delta P}{\rho}}$$

Used on every commercial aircraft.

## Practice Problems

<ExQ>
Water flows through a pipe that narrows from diameter 10 cm to 5 cm. The inlet velocity is 2 m/s and inlet pressure is 150 kPa. Find the velocity and pressure at the narrow section ($\rho = 1000$ kg/m³, same elevation).
</ExQ>
<ExA>
**Continuity:** $A_1 v_1 = A_2 v_2$

$$\frac{\pi(0.1)^2}{4}(2) = \frac{\pi(0.05)^2}{4} v_2 \implies v_2 = 2 \times \frac{(0.1)^2}{(0.05)^2} = \boxed{8\ \text{m/s}}$$

**Bernoulli** (same elevation):

$$P_2 = P_1 + \frac{1}{2}\rho(v_1^2 - v_2^2) = 150{,}000 + \frac{1}{2}(1000)(4 - 64) = 150{,}000 - 30{,}000 = \boxed{120\ \text{kPa}}$$
</ExA>

<ExQ>
A pitot tube on an aircraft measures a stagnation pressure of 101.8 kPa and static pressure of 100 kPa. Find the airspeed ($\rho_{\text{air}} = 1.225$ kg/m³).
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{2\Delta P}{\rho}} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 1800}{1.225}} = \sqrt{2939} \approx \boxed{54.2\ \text{m/s}} \approx 195\ \text{km/h}$$
</ExA>
