# Newton's Three Laws of Motion

Every mechanical engineering analysis ultimately rests on these three laws.

## First Law — Inertia

> An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion at constant velocity, unless acted upon by a net external force.

Mathematically: if $\sum \mathbf{F} = \mathbf{0}$, then $\mathbf{a} = \mathbf{0}$.

## Second Law — $F = ma$

$$\sum \mathbf{F} = m\mathbf{a}$$

The net force equals mass times acceleration. In component form:

$$\sum F_x = ma_x, \quad \sum F_y = ma_y, \quad \sum F_z = ma_z$$

This is the equation you solve in every dynamics problem.

## Third Law — Action-Reaction

> For every action there is an equal and opposite reaction.

If body A exerts force $\mathbf{F}$ on body B, then B exerts $-\mathbf{F}$ on A. Crucially, these forces act on **different bodies** — they never cancel each other in a single free body diagram.

## Free Body Diagram (FBD) Procedure

1. Isolate the body of interest
2. Draw all external forces: weight $mg\downarrow$, normal $N$, tension $T$, friction $f$
3. Choose a coordinate system aligned with motion
4. Apply $\sum F = ma$ in each direction
5. Solve the resulting equations

## Example: Block on an Incline

A 5 kg block sits on a frictionless incline of angle $\theta = 30°$.

Forces along the incline ($x$): $mg\sin\theta = ma$

$$a = g\sin 30° = 9.81 \times 0.5 = 4.9\ \text{m/s}^2$$

Forces perpendicular ($y$): $N - mg\cos\theta = 0 \implies N = mg\cos 30° = 42.4\ \text{N}$

## Practice Problems

<ExQ>
A 10 kg block is pulled horizontally with force $F = 50$ N across a surface with friction coefficient $\mu_k = 0.3$. Find the acceleration.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Normal force: $N = mg = 10 \times 9.81 = 98.1\ \text{N}$

Friction force: $f_k = \mu_k N = 0.3 \times 98.1 = 29.43\ \text{N}$

Newton's 2nd law (horizontal):
$$F - f_k = ma \implies 50 - 29.43 = 10a \implies a = \frac{20.57}{10} = \boxed{2.06\ \text{m/s}^2}$$
</ExA>

<ExQ>
Two blocks, $m_1 = 3$ kg and $m_2 = 5$ kg, are connected by a massless rope over a frictionless pulley (Atwood machine). Find the acceleration and the rope tension.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Take positive direction as $m_2$ moving down. Apply Newton's 2nd law to each block:

- Block 2 (down): $m_2 g - T = m_2 a$
- Block 1 (up): $T - m_1 g = m_1 a$

Adding both equations:
$$(m_2 - m_1)g = (m_1 + m_2)a \implies a = \frac{(5-3)}{(5+3)} \times 9.81 = \boxed{2.45\ \text{m/s}^2}$$

Tension:
$$T = m_1(g + a) = 3(9.81 + 2.45) = \boxed{36.8\ \text{N}}$$
</ExA>
