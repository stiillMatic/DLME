# Chapter 1 Practice — Kinematics

<ExQ>
**1.** Position $x(t) = 4t^2 - 3t + 2$ m. Find $v(t)$ and $a(t)$.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$v = 8t - 3$ m/s, $a = 8$ m/s² (constant).
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**2.** Car accelerates from 0 to 30 m/s in 6 s. Find acceleration and distance.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$a = 5$ m/s². $d = \tfrac{1}{2}(5)(36) = 90$ m.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**3.** Stone dropped from 80 m. Find time to hit ground and final speed.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$80 = \tfrac{1}{2}(9.81)t^2 \Rightarrow t = 4.04$ s. $v = 9.81\cdot 4.04 = 39.6$ m/s.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**4.** Projectile at 25 m/s, 60°. Find max height, range.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$h = (25\sin 60°)^2/(2\cdot 9.81) = 469/19.62 \approx 23.9$ m.

$R = (625\sin 120°)/9.81 \approx 55.2$ m.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**5. Conceptual.** A car driving in a circle at constant speed: is it accelerating?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
**Yes.** Velocity is a vector; constant speed but changing direction means $\mathbf{v}$ is changing → nonzero acceleration (centripetal, toward center).

This is why "speeding up" and "accelerating" are different in physics.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**6. Engineering.** Reaction time of a driver is 0.7 s. Car at 25 m/s. Compute total stopping distance assuming brake deceleration of 6 m/s².
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Reaction distance: $25\cdot 0.7 = 17.5$ m.

Braking distance: $25^2/(2\cdot 6) = 52.1$ m.

Total: $\approx 69.6$ m.
</ExA>
