# Chapter 4 Practice — Rotational Dynamics

<ExQ>
**1.** A wrench applies force 30 N perpendicular to a 0.25 m handle. Find torque.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\tau = rF\sin 90° = 0.25\cdot 30 = 7.5$ N·m.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**2.** A solid disk (2 kg, radius 0.3 m) spins at 10 rad/s. Find rotational KE.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$I = \tfrac{1}{2}MR^2 = 0.09$ kg·m².

$KE = \tfrac{1}{2}I\omega^2 = 4.5$ J.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**3.** Net torque 5 N·m on flywheel of moment of inertia 2 kg·m². Find angular acceleration.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\alpha = \tau/I = 5/2 = 2.5$ rad/s².
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**4. Conceptual.** Why does a spinning ice skater speed up when pulling arms in?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Conservation of angular momentum: $I\omega = $ const.

Pulling arms in reduces $I$ (less mass at large radius). To keep $I\omega$ constant, $\omega$ must increase. The skater spins faster.

Same principle: stars collapsing into neutron stars gain enormous spin rates.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**5. Multi-step.** A bowling ball (mass 7 kg, radius 0.1 m) rolls without slipping at 5 m/s. Find total kinetic energy.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Translational: $\tfrac{1}{2}(7)(25) = 87.5$ J.

Rolling without slip: $\omega = v/r = 50$ rad/s.

Solid sphere: $I = (2/5)MR^2 = 0.028$.

Rotational: $\tfrac{1}{2}(0.028)(2500) = 35$ J.

Total: $87.5 + 35 = 122.5$ J.

Note: the rotational fraction is $K_{\text{rot}}/K_{\text{total}} = 35/122.5 \approx 28.6\%$ — a significant portion in rolling problems.
</ExA>
