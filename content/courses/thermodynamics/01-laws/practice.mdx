# Chapter 1 Practice — Thermodynamic Laws

<ExQ>
**1.** A system absorbs 400 J of heat and does 150 J of work. Find $\Delta U$.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\Delta U = Q - W = 400 - 150 = 250$ J.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**2.** Ideal gas (1 mol, $C_v = 12.47$ J/mol·K) heated 50 K at constant volume. Find $Q$.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
At const V: $Q = \Delta U = nC_v\Delta T = 12.47\cdot 50 = 624$ J.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**3. Carnot efficiency.** Heat engine with $T_H = 600$ K, $T_L = 300$ K. Max efficiency?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\eta_{\text{max}} = 1 - 300/600 = 50\%$.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**4. Conceptual.** Why must heat flow from hot to cold spontaneously?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Second law: any spontaneous process must increase total entropy. Heat $Q$ from hot ($T_H$) to cold ($T_L$):

$\Delta S = -Q/T_H + Q/T_L = Q(1/T_L - 1/T_H) > 0$ when $T_L < T_H$.

Reverse direction: $\Delta S < 0$ — forbidden.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**5. Engineering.** A power plant rejects 60% of input heat. If input is 1000 MW, find work output and efficiency.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$Q_L = 600$ MW, $W = Q_H - Q_L = 400$ MW.

$\eta = 400/1000 = 40\%$.

(Compare to Carnot limit at typical 600/300 K = 50%.)
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**6. Multi-step.** Air ($\gamma = 1.4$) is compressed adiabatically from 300 K, 100 kPa to 800 kPa. Find $T_2$ and the specific work done on it.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$T_2 = 300\cdot(8)^{0.286} = 300\cdot 1.811 = 543$ K.

$w_{\text{on gas}} = -c_v\Delta T = -717\cdot(543-300)/1 = -174$ kJ/kg.

So about 174 kJ/kg of work is done ON the gas. (Specific heat of air $c_v \approx 717$ J/kg·K.)
</ExA>
