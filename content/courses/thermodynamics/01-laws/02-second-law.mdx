# The Second Law of Thermodynamics

The first law says energy is conserved. The second law says **not all energy conversions are equally possible** — there is a preferred direction for natural processes.

## Kelvin-Planck Statement

> It is impossible to construct a device that operates in a cycle and produces no effect other than the transfer of heat from a single reservoir and the production of work.

No heat engine can be 100% efficient. Some heat must always be rejected.

## Clausius Statement

> It is impossible to construct a device that operates in a cycle and produces no effect other than the transfer of heat from a cooler body to a hotter body.

Heat spontaneously flows from hot to cold — never the reverse without work input.

## Entropy

Entropy $S$ is a state property measuring molecular disorder. For a reversible process:

$$dS = \frac{\delta Q_{\text{rev}}}{T}$$

**Second law in terms of entropy:**

$$\Delta S_{\text{universe}} = \Delta S_{\text{system}} + \Delta S_{\text{surroundings}} \geq 0$$

Equality holds for reversible (ideal) processes. Real processes always increase total entropy.

## Carnot Efficiency

The Carnot cycle is the most efficient possible cycle operating between temperatures $T_H$ and $T_L$:

$$\eta_{\text{Carnot}} = 1 - \frac{T_L}{T_H}$$

where temperatures are in **Kelvin**. This sets the upper bound on all real heat engines.

**Example:** A steam turbine with $T_H = 600$ K and $T_L = 300$ K:

$$\eta_{\text{Carnot}} = 1 - \frac{300}{600} = 50\%$$

Real steam plants achieve 35–45%.

## Isentropic Processes

A reversible adiabatic process has $\Delta S = 0$ — it is **isentropic**. For ideal gases:

$$\frac{T_2}{T_1} = \left(\frac{P_2}{P_1}\right)^{(\gamma-1)/\gamma}, \quad \gamma = c_p/c_v$$

Turbines and compressors are analyzed as isentropic devices (with corrections for real losses via isentropic efficiency).

## Practice Problems

<ExQ>
A heat engine receives 500 kJ from a reservoir at 1000 K and rejects heat to a reservoir at 300 K. What is the maximum possible work output?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Maximum efficiency is Carnot efficiency:

$$\eta_{\text{Carnot}} = 1 - \frac{300}{1000} = 0.70 = 70\%$$

Maximum work:
$$W_{\text{max}} = \eta \times Q_H = 0.70 \times 500 = \boxed{350\ \text{kJ}}$$

Heat rejected: $Q_L = 500 - 350 = 150$ kJ.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
Air ($\gamma = 1.4$) is compressed isentropically from $T_1 = 300$ K, $P_1 = 100$ kPa to $P_2 = 800$ kPa. Find $T_2$.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$$T_2 = T_1 \left(\frac{P_2}{P_1}\right)^{(\gamma-1)/\gamma} = 300 \times \left(\frac{800}{100}\right)^{0.4/1.4}$$

$$= 300 \times (8)^{0.2857} = 300 \times 1.811 = \boxed{543\ \text{K}}$$
</ExA>
