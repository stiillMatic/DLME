# Chapter 4 Practice — Interference and Diffraction

<ExQ>
**1.** Two-slit: $d = 0.5$ mm, $L = 2$ m, $\lambda = 500$ nm. Fringe spacing?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\Delta y = \lambda L/d = (500\times 10^{-9})(2)/(5\times 10^{-4}) = 2\times 10^{-3}$ m = 2 mm.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**2.** Single slit $a = 50$ μm, $\lambda = 632.8$ nm (HeNe laser). First minimum?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\sin\theta = \lambda/a = 0.01266$. $\theta \approx 0.725°$.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**3.** Anti-reflective coating for $\lambda = 532$ nm (green laser), index 1.45?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$t = \lambda/(4n) = 532/(4\cdot 1.45) \approx 91.7$ nm.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**4. Engineering: Adaptive optics.** Mauna Kea telescope ($D = 10$ m) without adaptive optics has resolution ~1 arcsec due to seeing. With adaptive optics, what's the diffraction limit at $\lambda = 2$ μm (IR)?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\theta = 1.22\cdot 2\times 10^{-6}/10 = 2.44\times 10^{-7}$ rad ≈ 0.05 arcsec.

About 20× better than without AO. AO compensates for atmospheric turbulence in real time, recovering diffraction-limited performance.

This is why current ground-based observatories with AO rival or exceed Hubble in many wavelengths.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**5.** Diffraction grating with 1000 lines/mm. $\lambda = 600$ nm. First-order diffraction angle?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$d = 10^{-6}$ m. $\sin\theta_1 = \lambda/d = 0.6 \Rightarrow \theta_1 \approx 36.9°$.

Higher orders: $\sin\theta_m = m\lambda/d$. Only $m = 0, 1$ exist for this grating at this wavelength ($m = 2$ would need $\sin\theta > 1$).
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**6. Conceptual.** Why doesn't a perfectly transparent lens have zero reflectance?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
At a glass-air interface, reflection occurs whenever there's index mismatch (Fresnel reflection). Even perfect optical glass reflects ~4% per surface.

Anti-reflective coatings reduce this via interference. Multi-layer coatings can achieve <0.5% reflectance per surface across the visible spectrum. Used on camera lenses, telescope optics, eyeglasses.

Without AR coatings, multi-lens cameras would have severe ghost images and light loss.
</ExA>
