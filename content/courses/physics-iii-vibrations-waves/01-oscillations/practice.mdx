# Chapter 1 Practice — Oscillations

<ExQ>
**1.** A 2 kg mass on a spring with $k = 50$ N/m. Find angular frequency, period, and frequency.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\omega_0 = \sqrt{50/2} = 5$ rad/s. $T = 2\pi/5 \approx 1.257$ s. $f = 1/T \approx 0.796$ Hz.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**2.** An oscillator decays from amplitude 10 to 1 in 5 seconds. Find $\zeta\omega_0$.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$A(t) = A_0 e^{-\zeta\omega_0 t}$. $\ln(10) = 5\zeta\omega_0 \Rightarrow \zeta\omega_0 = \ln(10)/5 \approx 0.461$ s⁻¹.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**3.** Energy of SHM with amplitude 0.05 m, $k = 200$ N/m.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$E = \tfrac{1}{2}kA^2 = \tfrac{1}{2}(200)(0.0025) = 0.25$ J.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**4. Engineering: Quartz watch.** A quartz crystal oscillates at 32{,}768 Hz with $Q = 10^5$. Estimate how long the energy lasts.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Period: $1/32768 \approx 30.5$ μs.

$Q = \omega_0/(2\zeta\omega_0) = 1/(2\zeta) \Rightarrow \zeta = 5\times 10^{-6}$.

Decay rate: $\zeta\omega_0 \approx 5\times 10^{-6} \cdot 2\pi\cdot 32768 \approx 1.03$ s⁻¹.

Energy decays as $e^{-2\zeta\omega_0 t}\approx e^{-2t}$ — half-life around 0.35 s.

But the watch driver injects energy each cycle. The crystal "rings" for ~milliseconds after each pulse, providing the timing reference.

**Key insight:** high Q = sharp frequency selectivity, exactly what timekeeping needs.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**5.** Find the resonance peak frequency for $\zeta = 0.1$.
</ExQ>
<ExA>
$\omega_{\text{peak}} = \omega_0\sqrt{1 - 2\zeta^2} = \omega_0\sqrt{0.98} \approx 0.99\omega_0$.

Very close to $\omega_0$ for light damping.
</ExA>

<ExQ>
**6. Conceptual.** Why are tuning forks made of high-Q materials?
</ExQ>
<ExA>
Tuning fork must produce a clean, pure tone of definite frequency. High Q = sharp resonance, sustained ringing.

If Q were low (heavily damped), the fork would emit a muffled "thud" with broad frequency spread — useless for tuning.

Same principle: musical instruments (strings, drums) all carefully selected to have appropriate Q for sustaining their characteristic note.
</ExA>
